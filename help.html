HOW TO HELP OTHERS

Pick a good time. Choose a time when you can speak to the person in private at a time of emotional calm in a comfortable environment.

Be prepared for denial and resistance. There's a good chance your loved one may deny having an eating disorder or become angry and defensive. Try to remain calm, focused, and respectful.

Avoid giving simple solutions. For example, “All you have to do is accept yourself.” Eating disorders are complex problems. If it were that easy, the person wouldn't be suffering.
